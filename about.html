The backstory.

Agile was introduced for customers. Customers are human. Those who deliver are human too. So where did we drift to product only?

What agile actually said

Agile never started as a delivery machine.

Talk to each other. Trust the people doing the work. Stay close to the customer. Ship, learn, adjust.

The Manifesto put people on the left on purpose. Individuals over process. Collaboration over contracts. Software only matters when humans are still in the room.

I. For customers

Agile was introduced to serve people better. Listen sooner. Ship what matters. The customer was the reason.

II. Customers are human

Not a persona slide. Not a conversion rate. A person with patience, frustration, and trust to earn.

III. Deliverers are human too

The people who build and ship are not capacity units. They sit in the ceremonies. They absorb the timeline. That was always part of the deal.

IV. The drift

We kept the shipping part.

We kept the dashboards, the frameworks, the rollouts, the timelines.

We turned customers into metrics. We turned teams into velocity. We turned progress into output.

The customer was still on the slide. The team was still in the standup. But the human left the center.

We did not abandon agile. We abandoned the humans it was written for.

Human Agile

Human Agile is the answer. Put both humans back. The one who receives the product. The one who delivers it.

Not more process. Not another transformation on a calendar. Both sides of the promise, from design through scale.

V. Why we exist

Agile Fragile names the drift and builds Human Agile for organizations that are ready to put both humans back in the room.
