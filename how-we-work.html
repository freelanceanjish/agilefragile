01 · The gap

Most transformation proposals start with tooling and timelines. This one starts with what is happening to your people.

Start with the social system, not the slide deck

A proposal that ignores socio-technical tension will repeat the harm you are trying to fix.

Most transformation proposals optimize the technical system: ceremonies, tooling, reporting, scale patterns.

The social system (trust, safety, learning, belonging) gets treated as change management wallpaper. Human Agile inverts that order. The Index and honest interviews read the social system first. Only then does it make sense to talk about workshops, community, or resources.

05 · Response

Three directions the proposal could take after the social system is read honestly. Not a catalogue. A response to the harm you read in step one.

Restore

Workshops

For teams recovering from Dark Agile. Reset trust before you rebuild.

  • Diagnostic on what is crushing people
  • Strip ceremony theater and dashboard judgment
  • Rebuild psychological safety with leaders in the room

Support

Community

Ongoing peer space for practitioners and leaders mid-transformation.

  • Honest conversation, not performative agile talk
  • Peers who have seen Dark Agile transformations up close
  • Shared language from the Human Agile model

Sustain

Resources

Tools and playbooks that cut admin overhead and keep humans at the center.

  • Lightweight rituals teams can actually keep
  • Leader guides for enablement over tracking
  • Index re-checks as you scale
