Human is the architecture

Our mission is to put humans back at the center of enterprise agile. Simply put: if agility does not work for the people inside the organization, it does not work. This guides the Human Agile model, the Index, and every field note published here.

We are building a correction for when agility is performed on dashboards instead of practiced with humans inside. A lens and a sequence of moves you run before and during adoption. Not a replacement for Scrum or SAFe. A human gate for when the rollout looks green and people are breaking.

Our story

Agile was created to help organizations. Organizations are made of humans. Across coaching, implementation, and scaling at enterprise level, the pattern repeats: people are the least trained, least attended, and least focused part of the transformation.

We did not set out to invent another framework. We watched agility get performed on dashboards for leadership while engineers burned out, retros became tracking sessions, and the best people quietly left. That is not agile failure. It is human failure dressed in agile language.

The Human Agile model started as field notes from that pattern: a human gate before scale, four moves (Human, Adapt, Implement, Scale), and an Index that scores human practice only. High delivery with low human scores is still Dark Agile.

Agile Fragile is where those notes live publicly. If the model resonates with what you see in your organization, we can talk. Proposed by Anjish Bhondwe.
